Overview

Most cloud cost analysis stops at reporting. This engine goes a step further by connecting infrastructure drivers to pricing, total cost, and business metrics so organizations can understand not only what they spent, but how cost behaves as demand changes.

Why It Matters

FinOps becomes more strategic when cost is translated into operational and business terms. Instead of reacting after spend lands, teams can evaluate how architecture, demand growth, and usage behavior influence cost-to-serve.

How the Model Works

The model follows a simple logic chain from technical usage signals to business-facing cost insight.

1. Capture usage drivers

Baseline consumption is collected for infrastructure elements such as compute hours, database hours, object storage, or API/request volume.

2. Apply pricing assumptions

Rates are assigned to each driver to translate raw infrastructure usage into cost, whether from public cloud pricing, internal rates, or modeled assumptions.

3. Translate into business insight

Total cost is connected to business metrics like customers, transactions, or orders to produce usable unit economics and scenario-based decision support.

Core outcome: This engine helps connect engineering activity to financial impact, making cost behavior easier to explain, forecast, and manage.
